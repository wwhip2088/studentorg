Thurtene





Tradition and History

The main purpose of ThurtenE Carnival is to bring together the Washington University and Greater St. Louis communities for three days of exhilarating rides, live performances, and delicious food. It is the largest and oldest student-run carnival in the nation, organized by members of ThurtenE Honorary at Washington University. As is pertains to AEPi, every year, fraternities and sororities at Washington University pair up to create facades for Thurtene. During Thurtene weekend, each fraternity and sorority is judged on their spirit, play, and construction, as well as their overall performance. Each year, a committee of Thurtene overalls judge each pairing on these qualities.

Our 2015 Facade





Welcome to Our City Block

For the past several years, Alpha Epsilon Pi has been paired with Pi Beta Phi. This year, our chosen theme was that of a City Block. The facade featured several super-structures, including models of a bodega, a subway sign, a bookstore, a pizza shop, a deli, a "boomingdales", and the off-campus apartments where many senior members of AEPi and PiPhi reside. For our efforts, the AEPiBetaPhi team took home the overall award called The Burmeister, as well as the Spirit award.
